Climate Risk on Zillow


Source: The Verge


Climate change* has been, well, changing the climate. Wildfires, hurricanes, and extreme heat are notable effects the world has been experiencing these past few years.
*If you do not believe in climate change, talk to me.

A new feature on the real estate listing app Zillow has now been a result of the world's changing conditions. Climate risk outlines the environmental risks associated with listed properties on Zillow.

Susceptibility to wildfires, flooding, extreme temperatures, high winds, and poor air quality will be featured on the app in conjunction with required or recommended insurances to purchase for your potential home.

Data is provided through a partnership with First Street, a firm specializing in Climate Risk Financial Modeling (yay FinTech). The hope is to provide home buyers with more information as they consider listings. According to The Verge, new Zillow listings in August were 16.7 percent at major risk of wildfire and 12.8 percent of listings had major risk of flooding.

This news is especially relevant to me because my neighborhood, Rancho Palos Verdes, deals with constant land shifts. LA County's sudden spurts of heavy rain makes the ground unable to absorb all the water which causes landslides that affect people's properties.
